# HadleyRead — Teaching Programme

This document contains the complete teaching programme for HadleyRead.
It is used by the server to deliver rule introductions and select practice words.
All rule introduction scripts are hardcoded — they are passed directly to TTS, not generated by GPT-4o.

---

## How This Document Is Used

- Rule introduction scripts are spoken once per pattern per session
- Practice words are selected by the engine from the word lists below
- The engine never deviates from the phase/pattern order without a specific progression trigger
- GPT-4o does not see this document — it receives only the current pattern name and rule summary

---

## Phase 1 — Short Vowels

Rule: a vowel between two consonants makes a short sound.

---

### Pattern: Short A

**Rule introduction script:**
"Some short words have a vowel in the middle that makes a quick, flat sound. When you see the letter A between two consonants — like in cat or van — it makes that short A sound. Not the name of the letter. Just a quick, open sound. Listen — cat. van. tap. Now you try."

**Practice words — isolated context:**
bag, van, tap, mat, cap, ran, flat, clap, trap, man, fan, rat, hat, jam, lap, nap, back, rack, lack, wax

**Sentence context examples:**
- "Pack your bag."
- "The van is here."
- "Tap the screen."
- "Clap your hands."

**Exception words at this level:** none

---

## Phase 2 — Consonant Blends

Rule: two consonants together — say both sounds, then read the rest.

---

### Pattern: S Blends

**Rule introduction script:**
"Some words start with two consonants together. When S is followed by another consonant you say both sounds — st. sp. sl. Then read the rest of the word. Listen — stop. step. slip. Now you try."

**Practice words — isolated context:**
stop, step, slip, skill, span, strap, split, spray, skin, spin, spit, spot, snag, snap, swim, slim, sled, slug, spec, spud

**Sentence context examples:**
- "Stop at the sign."
- "Step back."
- "Spray the screen."

---

### Pattern: L Blends

**Rule introduction script:**
"When a consonant is followed by L at the start of a word, say both sounds together — fl. cl. pl. Then read the rest. Listen — flat. clip. plan. Now you try."

## Phase 4 — Magic E

## Re-teach Scripts

Used by the engine when a pattern is not landing after multiple attempts.
Different angle, different words, same rule.

**Short A re-teach:**
"Let's try this a different way. The A in the middle — say it on its own first. Just — a. Now add the sounds around it. C — a — t. Cat. Try it that way."

**Short I re-teach:**
"Break it down. Say the first sound. Then the I in the middle — i. Then the last sound. Put them together slowly."

**Digraph SH re-teach:**
"Forget the two letters for a second. Just make the sound on its own — sh. Now put it at the start of the word and read the rest."

**Magic E re-teach:**
"Cover the E at the end with your finger. Read what's left. Now take your finger away and let the E change the middle sound."

**Blend re-teach:**
"Say the first two sounds on their own — st. sp. gr. Now add the rest of the word to the end."
